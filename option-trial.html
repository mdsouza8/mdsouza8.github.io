eTable 1 Representativeness Notes
⚠ Generalizability Considerations
All sites in China; ~99% Han Chinese. Male overrepresentation common in Chinese stroke trials. Asian patients have higher rates of large artery atherosclerosis vs cardioembolic etiology compared to Western populations. Non-LVO represents 54–76% of all ischemic stroke patients globally.
eFigure 1 Patients Enrolled per Center — Top 20
50 centers total. Top 2 centers (Linyi 112, Chenzhou 80) contributed 34% of all patients. Trial ran June 2023 – August 2025.
⚠️
Critical Imaging Considerations
⚠ NCCT Hypodensity
21 patients had CT hypodensities larger than CTP-defined core — 2 developed sICH. Careful NCCT evaluation is essential before proceeding.
⚠ Posterior Circulation
CTP thresholds less well validated posteriorly. Trial showed homogeneous benefit in both circulations — apply clinical judgment for PCA/cerebellar territory.
⚠ Stenosis Without Occlusion
~23% of trial had stenosis (no occlusion) as qualifying lesion. This population drove much of the positive result — degree of stenosis not reported; unclear applicability to mild stenosis.
Heidelberg Bleeding Classification Spectrum
Definite + Probable → Symptomatic. Possible + Unlikely → Asymptomatic. Primary sICH definition used Heidelberg.

Key Takeaway
Extended-window TNK (4.5–24 hrs) significantly improved excellent functional outcomes (mRS 0–1) with RR 1.28, NNT = 11 (p = 0.02) in patients with non-LVO stroke, favorable CTP mismatch, and no EVT eligibility. The ~23% of patients with stenosis (no occlusion) were included and likely contributed to the positive ITT result — the MVO-only subgroup just missed significance (p=0.054).  ⚠ Significantly increased sICH: 2.8% vs 0% (p=0.004). Any ICH 14.6% vs 9.5%.  90-day mortality was not significantly different (5.0% vs 3.2%, HR 1.59, CI 0.69–3.67, p=0.28), but 5 of 14 TNK deaths were from ICH versus 0 of 9 in standard care.
