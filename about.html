Hi! I'm Marissa.

I'm a fourth-year medical student with passions for neuroscience, technology, and the humanities.

Ever since attending "Brain Camp" at Georgia Tech when I was 10 years old and typing my first HTML code at around the same age, I have remained intrigued by the nervous system and technology. A lover of logic puzzles, the STEM disciplines, and objectivity, I enjoy problem solving and figuring out why things work the way they do. Equally strong, however, is the side of me that values creativity, design, and the humanities. What I love most about the field of Neuroscience is its interdisciplinary nature, integrating medicine, philosophy, technology, and sociology in a way that allows me to explore all my various interests.

I have wanted to be a doctor since that very same Brain Camp as it would let me work with people, employ my curiousity, and excercise my critical thinking (& get paid for doing so!). However, I wanted to do so with a tecnological perspective in mind, and so I went on to obtain an undergraduate degree in Computer Science from Georgia Tech. There, I learned to design, build, and evaluate human-centered systems. I am now in my final year of medical school, hoping to continue my education at a Neurology residency program next year. I have also been lucky enough to travel to various countries, learning about people from various cultures/backgrounds and strengthening my passion for #WhatMakesUsHuman. With so much left unknown about the nervous system, I hope to one day combine my medical and technological knowledge to create accessible, aesthetically-pleasing diagnostic tools & therapies for those with neurologic and neuropsychiatric conditions. I have put together this website as a means to both express myself and to organize my thoughts & creations. Enjoy!
